“A better world shall emerge based on faith and understanding” -General Douglas Macarthur
Strife has fractured the Korean peninsula for years. Years of dispute and bloodshed, combined with divisive policies have reduced almost all hope for amity in the region. A once united and proud people subjugated cruelly under the yoke of Japanese imperialism now find themselves divided. For 70 years discord has continued to grow among the nations. As the DPRK continues to flex its military and nuclear muscles, the US and her allies show no signs of backing down. With new players entering the ring, it is anyone’s game. The border has seen its fair share of incursions, forced propaganda and its ultimate demilitarization. But with the situation only resting on the knife’s edge it may quick divulge into war. As the delegates of the Security Council, it is of utmost importance that you not only solve problems at hand but set a precedent for similar situations henceforth. Committee will progress in the continual crisis format, so delegates, be prepared for quick thinking and careful strategic evaluations. Prepare for all out war.

Topic A: Assessing the threat of Lethal Autonomous Weapons "Individual science fiction stories may seem as trivial as ever to the blinder critics and philosophers of today - but the core of science fiction, its essence has become crucial to our salvation if we are to be saved at all.”-Issac Asimov A laser wielding android threatening and overpower its defenceless human adversaries has so far only been witnessed in fiction, but it might not be too far from the truth. Advanced robots capable of engaging a human target autonomously will no longer be confined to fiction; they are rapidly becoming a reality. 'Killer Robots' - or, officially, 'Lethal Autonomous Weapons' (LAWs), are robots that - once activated - can operate without any further human intervention. This new weapons system will be capable of identifying and attacking a human target on its own. So delegates could this potentially reduce the number of casualties of war or potentially be the start of an entirely different war?
Topic B:The Militarisation of Space
The Earth’s orbit is looking more and more like the planet’s surface — heavily armed and primed for war. A war in orbit could wreck the delicate satellite constellations that the world relies on for navigation, communication, scientific research and military surveillance. Widespread orbital destruction could send humanity through a technological time warp. The Militarisation of space could potentially entail a new age in modern warfare and it is up to you delegates to direct the potential beginning of a whole new arms race.

It is said that there is but a fine line between artistry and insanity, between love and hate, between war and peace and of course, the fine line between India and Pakistan. Border disputes have become short of a phenomenon between the two nations today- from territorial incursions, to human right violations, to drone insecurity, to non state activity- there seems to be no end. The Indo-Pak territorial dispute over the State of Jammu and Kashmir is one as old as both the nation's themselves- stemming into one of the most terse bilateral scenarios globally. Amidst the insurgents, the ceasefires, the hostility and several thousands dead; amidst the wars of 1945, 1967, 1999 and more, only one thing had distinguished itself from the haze of hate and distrust: the need to solve the Issue of Kashmir. If that wasn't enough delegates, the burden of resolving border conflicts between the two nations now lies in the hands of a joint cabinet of Indian and Pakistani ministers. Welcome delegates to the Joint Cabinet meeting of India and Pakistan, 2025! It is now in your hands to cease the crumpled paper of trust between the two nations, and smoothen bilateral ties between them. Let's hope that history has truly reaped lessons learnt well because, like Murphy said, the worst outcome that can happen, will happen. Cross your heart delegates, and hope to dear God that your worst is good, good enough.

"If you owe your bank a hundred pounds you have a problem but if you owe a million it has one.”- J.M Keynes
With the European Union’s priorities being torn between its own financial hurdles and the situation of refugees from across its shores, the lives of millions lie in such few hands. Despite having received billions in bailout, Greece’s economic problems are far from over. With most of the money in the new package to be used to repay existing debts, the current focus must be brought back to rebuilding the shattered European economy. While some blame austerity measures for having created an apparent humanitarian crisis in Greece, others blame Athens for failing to have executed the required norms of the bailout. You as delegates of the ECOFIN will have to rise above the blame- game and provide well- thought out solutions to help the EU overcome this catastrophic crisis challenging its unity, as well as bring the refugee situation under control. It is up to you to question the current scenario- How did it all boil down to this point? What if Greece left the Eurozone? Moreover, it is up to you to provide the answers…and save the world from possible financial doom. Agenda: The Eurozone Crisis with special reference to the Greek Bailout, the future of the EU, and the refugee crisis.
Background guides: DISEC, EU, UNSC, JC
